Why Halter

This role found me.
Here's why I care.

I've spent the last year looking for work where the mission and the business model are the same thing. Where the product itself is the impact. Halter is that company.

01

The mission is real

600,000+ collars deployed. Farmers spending more time with their families. Livestock welfare measurably improved. When I tell people about Halter, they lean in. That reaction tells me everything about the product and the people behind it.

02

I bring both sides

Most applicants will lean towards people experience or product thinking. I bring both. I've designed HR reports for boards at GNS Science, administered the Kronos HRIS at MFG Companies, and written the onboarding handbook from scratch because new hires were arriving without one. Separately, I built Yarn's entire product ecosystem as sole owner. That combination is what this role needs.

03

I think in ecosystems

Most people see an HRIS, an ATS, a performance platform. I see data flows, lifecycle states, and decision points. My instinct is to connect systems into a 360° view. Disconnected systems create invisible friction that compounds as you scale into Australia and the US.

04

I start with what people feel

My counselling background taught me to understand what someone actually needs before reaching for a solution. At GNS Science, that meant designing engagement surveys that surfaced real feelings about wellbeing and culture. At Yarn, it meant building an app around conversations people needed to have with the people they love. You can't build a good people ecosystem without that empathy.

05

AI is where I'm already heading

I led the AI committee at 2degrees, deploying applications across customer service and internal operations. My Master's thesis explored AI's impact on education. The question of how AI can make people operations more humane is one I think about a lot. I'm ready to act on it at Halter.

06

I get energy from building

Yarn was built to profitability with no team, no paid acquisition, and no external funding. I get more satisfaction from removing a step than adding one. "This isn't easy, and we love that it's hard." I read that on Halter's careers page and it just clicked. That's the environment where I do my most meaningful work.
